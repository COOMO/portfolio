Portfolio / Product case study 作品集 / 產品案例分析 ▮ A POS for a family bakery 為家庭麵包店做的 POS

A POS, built for
family. 寫給家人的
POS。

A small bakery run by my in-laws. Once business took off, the handwritten order book on the counter became the bottleneck — missed pickups, phone-tag, no way to dig up last week's order from "that gentleman." This POS is built for the two of them: Rust + Dioxus, one codebase across Android / iPad / Windows, local-first, every action undoable. Every feature, every button size, even the on-screen keyboard's surname ordering — all tailored to what the two of them actually need at the counter. 岳父岳母經營的麵包店。生意爆紅之後，櫃台那本手寫的訂單簿變成瓶頸， 漏取件、追電話、找不到上週訂走的那位先生的訂單。 這個 POS 是為了幫助他們兩人：Rust + Dioxus 一份 codebase 跑在 Android / iPad / Windows，本地優先、操作可還原。 每一個功能、按鈕的尺寸、鍵盤的姓氏排序，都是為了岳父岳母的需求量身訂做。

§ 01 · Brief § 01 · 動機

Why replace a paper order book? 為什麼要取代一本訂單簿？

The shop runs on Facebook and IG. Orders arrive by phone, by Messenger, by IG DM, by walk-in — all funneled to one notebook on the counter. As word spread, the notebook became the bottleneck. Three specific pains drove the rewrite, and each one maps to a deliberate product decision. 這間店靠 Facebook 和 IG 接單。訂單從電話、Messenger、IG 私訊、現場散客四方湧入， 全部寫進櫃台上一本筆記簿。生意越好，那本簿子越是瓶頸。 有三個具體的痛點推動了這次重寫，每一個都對應到一條明確的產品決策。

Pain · 01 痛點 · 01
The paper ledger can't be searched. 手寫訂單簿難以搜尋。
Hurried scribbles get misread. Reading glasses on to verify a phone number and a bread count, with a long queue waiting behind for their bread — that's the daily reality, and the daily stress. 匆匆忙忙記下的潦草字跡常常在查詢訂單時看走眼，戴上老花眼鏡確認電話號碼和麵包數量， 後面還有長長的隊伍等待拿麵包，是日常、也是壓力。
─── replaced with ──▶ ─── 換成 ──▶
Previously: find an order = finger-scan multiple pages and squint.

Now: search matches name fuzzy and phone prefix in one go; the UI floors at 18 pt text and 48 × 48 px touch targets, high contrast; the on-screen keyboard sorts surnames by in-shop frequency, so 林/陳/王/張 sit at the top. Finding an order is one tap, not ten. 以往：訂單寫在簿子上，找一筆要靠手指掃過幾頁、再對眼。

現在：搜尋同時比對姓名模糊與電話前綴；UI 字級 ≥ 18 pt、 觸控 ≥ 48 × 48 px、高對比；自訂螢幕鍵盤按店內姓氏出現頻率排序， 林/陳/王/張永遠在第一排。找一筆是一個動作，不是十個。
Pain · 02 痛點 · 02
Always counting bread, never agreeing. 總是在清點麵包數量。
After scrambling to find the entry and ship the order, the new bake comes out — wait, the flavor count doesn't add up. Recount stock, recount demand, realize the order book wasn't updated, find the new total disagreeing with the previous one. That's when the tempers behind the counter start to flare. 一陣手忙腳亂後找到資料、出貨，新烤的麵包出爐，奇怪了，怎麼口味數量又對不上？ 重新清點庫存和需求，忘記更新訂單簿又發現怎麼跟上一輪算的不一樣， 此時此刻火藥味就出現了。
─── collapsed into ──▶ ─── 收斂為 ──▶
Previously: the order book is one ledger, the prep list another, the cooling tray a third — three sources, always chasing each other.

Now: orders, kiln batches, and walk-in stock all live in the same SQLite — one source of truth. stock_service recomputes available quantity per flavor on every new order; a toast warns when an order exceeds today's batches but never blocks the save (the owners have the final word). The moment a fresh bake lands in walk-in stock, the whole screen reconciles. No second ledger, no second count. 以往：訂單簿是一份資料、清單是另一份、剛烤好的盤子是第三份， 三邊永遠在追對方。

現在：訂單、窯次計劃、現場散客庫存（walk_in_stock） 全寫進同一份 SQLite，只有一份真相。 stock_service 即時算出每口味的可供應量； 超量時跳 toast 警告，但不擋存單（店主有最終決定權）。 剛烤好的麵包記進現場庫存的那一刻，整個畫面同步重算。 不再有第二本帳，不再有第二次清點。
Pain · 03 痛點 · 03
No way to see next week at a glance. 難以預估未來要預留的人手和備料。
Flip the order book open — some days already carry over a hundred bread orders. A new order rings in; on the phone, my mother-in-law leafs page by page to find a day with headroom, then asks the customer to hold. There's no at-a-glance way to see which days still have capacity to spare. 翻開訂單簿，某些日子會有上百條麵包需求。這時新的訂單來了， 電話中岳母得打開訂單簿，一頁一頁的翻到還有空檔的營業日， 再向客人確認時間。目前沒有一個一目了然的方式讓他們知道 哪些日子有多餘的產能能分配。
─── flattened to ──▶ ─── 攤平為 ──▶
Previously: future load lives in pages of the order book — you only know after flipping.

Now: the new-order flow opens a pressure-coloured calendar tagging each day's total bread count in four bands (light · medium · tight · overload), with ⚠ for non-business days — at a glance from the moment the phone rings. The 窯次 tab also flattens the next several bake-days into a demand heatmap: columns are dates, rows are flavors, cell colour is ordered quantity. No more flipping pages mid-call; no more spreadsheet wrestling at prep time. 以往：未來幾天的負載藏在訂單簿一頁一頁裡，只有翻過才知道。

現在：新增訂單時的取件日月曆 用四級壓力色標每天的麵包量（鬆 / 中 / 緊 / 爆）， 非營業日加 ⚠ 標記，接電話那一刻就看得到哪天還有空檔。 窯次分頁再把未來幾個出爐日攤平成 需求熱圖：欄是日期、列是口味、顏色是已訂數量。 接電話時不再翻頁；備料時不再對表格。

§ 03 · System § 03 · 系統架構

Three layers. One binary. Five platforms. 三層架構。一份 binary。五個平台。

A single Rust workspace targets Android, iOS, iPad, Windows, and Linux from one codebase. Strictly layered: UI talks to services, services talk to the DB, sync runs on its own thread and never touches UI. The UI thread never awaits a DB call — every database operation flows through a use_coroutine channel or spawn_blocking. 一個 Rust workspace 從同一份程式碼出 Android / iOS / iPad / Windows / Linux。 嚴格分層：UI 呼叫 services、services 呼叫 DB、sync 跑在自己的執行緒上、 完全不碰 UI。UI 執行緒絕不 await DB 呼叫， 所有資料庫操作都走 use_coroutine 或 spawn_blocking。

§ 04 · By design § 04 · 設計重點

User-first, test-driven. 使用者優先，測試驅動。

Two stances drove every design call. User-first asked does this make the counter easier? before any other question. Test-driven meant each service function landed in the same commit as its tests, order_service, undo_service, kiln_service, stock_service all carry inline mod tests blocks that re-verify the four properties below on every change. 兩個立場貫穿每個設計決策。使用者優先先問 這讓櫃台更輕鬆嗎？，其餘的問題排後面。 測試驅動意指每個 service 函數跟它的測試在同一個 commit 出貨， order_service、undo_service、kiln_service、 stock_service 都帶著 inline mod tests， 每次變更都重新驗證下面這四個特性。

Operator-first UX 以操作者為先的 UX

18 pt floor. Three taps to anywhere. 字級不小於 18 pt。任何頁面三步到達。

Font size minima are const &strs in src/ui/theme.rs — 18 pt for body, 20 pt for buttons, 24 pt for titles. Touch targets are 48 px minimum, 64 × 56 for primary CTAs. No nested dropdowns: every selection is a grid of large cells. The custom on-screen keyboard renders surname-frequency-sorted initials so 林/陳/王/張 sit at the top — typing a name is one tap, not three. 字級最小值都是 src/ui/theme.rs 裡的 const &str， 內文 18 pt、按鈕 20 pt、標題 24 pt。觸控目標最小 48 px， 主要 CTA 64 × 56。沒有層層下拉：所有選擇都是大格子。 自製的螢幕鍵盤把姓氏依 店內資料庫的出現頻率排序， 林/陳/王/張 永遠在最上面，輸入姓名是一個動作、不是三個。

Failure mode prevented: the in-laws abandon the app and go back to the notebook because a five-step flow is harder than picking up a pen. 防止的故障：岳父岳母放棄 app、回去翻筆記簿， 因為一個五步驟的流程比拿起筆來得難。

Local-first storage 離線優先儲存

Save first. Sync later. Never block on the cloud. 先存到本地。慢慢同步。雲端不擋路。

SQLite is opened with journal_mode=WAL and foreign_keys=ON on every platform. Primary keys are UUID v4 stored as TEXT — built for the day a second device joins. Cloud sync is a periodic snapshot upload: WAL checkpoint → copy the DB file → upload to Drive/iCloud → record last sync time. It runs on app pause and on a 10-minute timer, and a failure shows a quiet ⚠ in the corner — nothing else. 每個平台上 SQLite 都用 journal_mode=WAL + foreign_keys=ON 打開。主鍵是 UUID v4，存成 TEXT， 為「未來有第二台裝置進來」鋪好路。雲端同步是定期的 快照上傳：WAL checkpoint → 複製 DB 檔 → 上傳 Drive/iCloud → 記錄上次同步時間。 App 進背景時觸發、每 10 分鐘也跑一次。失敗時角落安靜地顯示 ⚠，僅此而已。

Failure mode prevented: an order lost because the Wi-Fi dropped mid-save. The two of them see the spinner finish locally before the cloud is even asked. 防止的故障：單存到一半 Wi-Fi 斷了就消失。 岳父岳母看到 spinner 在本地存完，才輪到雲端那邊去打招呼。

Undoable everything 每件事都能還原

Every mutation writes its own reversal. 每一個變更都自帶反向操作。

Every state-mutating service call (create order, cancel, mark picked-up, update stock, change payment) writes a row to operation_logs carrying both a payload_json (do) and a reverse_json (undo). One tap on the undo bar runs the reverse in the same transaction — no shadow tables, no soft delete, no "are you sure" mid-flow. The architecture codifies forgiveness: nothing they do is final until they walk away from the tablet. 每一個會改資料的 service 呼叫（建單、取消、取件、改庫存、改付款） 都會寫一列 operation_logs， 同時帶 payload_json（do）跟 reverse_json（undo）。 按一下 undo bar、反向操作在同一個 transaction 跑， 沒有影子表、沒有 soft delete、流程中不再問「您確定嗎」。 這個架構把「原諒」寫進了程式裡： 他們做的每件事，在離開那台平板前都還不算定案。

Failure mode prevented: a wrong tap that can't be taken back. Two 60-something owners' confidence in a new tool is built one undo at a time. 防止的故障：誤觸了就回不去。 兩位 60+ 歲經營者對新工具的信心，是一次一次按 undo 堆出來的。

Multi-tenant ready 多商家就緒

Shop name, colours, flavors — all data, not code. 店名、顏色、口味，全是資料，不是程式碼。

The shop name appears in zero hardcoded string literals. Every brand surface reads from tenant_settings at startup: shop name, primary colour, logo path, currency, reminder lead time. The flavor catalogue is a SQL table. CLAUDE.md enshrines the rule ("❌ let shop_name = "..." — hardcode 禁止！"). Re-skinning the app for the next family bakery is a tenant_settings UPDATE plus flavors INSERTs. 店名沒有出現在任何 hardcoded 字串裡。 所有品牌相關介面啟動時從 tenant_settings 讀取： 店名、主色、logo 路徑、幣別、提醒前置時間。 口味目錄是一張 SQL 表。CLAUDE.md 把規則寫死了 （「❌ let shop_name = "..." — hardcode 禁止！」）。 重新上架給下一間家庭麵包店 = 改一列 tenant_settings + 插幾筆 flavors。

Failure mode prevented: the moment a second shop signs up and you need to grep the bakery name across the codebase to find every hardcoded string. There isn't one. 防止的故障：第二間店簽下來那一刻， 你才開始 grep 店名找寫死的字串。沒得找，一個都沒有。
